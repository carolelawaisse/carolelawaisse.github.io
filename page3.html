About Traveling

As I am still studying, I can't leave for too long.
But me and my boyfriend try to travel as
much as we can. We make a lot of small trips
and once a year, we try to go to the other side
of our planet. This year we went to Ecuador.
Next stop? Bolivia.

'Why does everybody always say 'and then we go home'?'

-Mabelle Frias

Our trip last year was to Ecuador. In just one month we have tried to discover as much of the country as possible.
The first few days we had to do it quietly as we were incredibly weak because of the altitude. Altitude sickness cannot be explained but is something you have to experience in order to understand it. Going up the stairs becomes as heavy because of the altitude sickness as going to jog for half an hour. But that did not stop us from sniffing up culture completely. For example, we were given a private guided tour of Quito by a local friend. Once we had all those typical tourist attractions behind us, we left on a night bus and boat towards Cyabeno National Reserve (a protected area of the Amazon forest). Where night and day walks, alligator spotting and local peoples were waiting for us. We also met a new friend there. Exhausted we returned to Quito where we stayed only one day before we left for our next stop, Mindo. A nice stop when you're looking for a cosy atmosphere and a slightly less intense jungle. After three days in Mindo, we took the bus to Otavalo, where we stayed in the most cosy hostel so far. We got to know one of the nicest families. The two children were incredibly clever just like their parents and also traveling the world and staying together was the most important thing. We were impressed by the large markets, but especially the Cuichocha lake. The quiet atmosphere and colours blew me over completely and were one of the highlights of the trip for me.
After a nice farewell to the family, we left for Latacunga. From where we could visit both Quilotoa and the Cotopaxi. Two completely different but incredible volcanoes. This was the highlight of the trip for my friend as he was able to climb the second largest volcano in the world.
Puerto Lopez was the opposite, making my friend ill for two days as we have changed altitude and climate throughout the journey. But the urge to spot humpback whales soon made him feel better again. The whales were a perfect finale for our wonderful journey. Even before we were back in Belgium, we were already busy deciding where our next trip would take us.
